The system

Two surfaces, one arc

Common Work is the coordination surface — a peer commons where shared work moves through six stages from captured idea to resolved outcome. Each stage carries its own colour from the sunrise spectrum, making the state of collective effort visible at a glance. The field view renders the whole commons as a concentric map: work at the center, peers in the rings, signals on the periphery.

The Daybook is the personal daily record — a module within the cooperative's Common Information System where a member's lived activity enters the ledger one entry at a time. It adds a ripening lifecycle between the moment something happens and the moment it becomes an immutable event, and keeps that move in the member's hands. Nothing economic is written without a person landing it.

The load-bearing rule A precondition set can reach one hundred percent, and the signal can show ready, but only a person's landing action writes the authoritative event. Every economic write in the Daybook has a named human author at the moment of commit.

Daybook entry types

Four kinds, one landing rule

Three kinds compose economic events the CIS already holds. The fourth — Reflection — is the one new thing: narrative, non-valued, private by default. Everything economic requires a human to land it before it touches a balance.

Reflection
Narrative, non-valued. Private to the author until explicitly made public. Append-only, portable, censorship-resistant. The one new entity the module adds to the CIS.
Contribution
Labor logged. Member reads and confirms, then lands. Emits a capital_event against the program budget. Self-confirmed.
Exchange
Transfer or allocation of shared value. Brought to the circle; requires the circle's assent before landing. Higher bar because it moves shared value.
Clearing
Settlement between vaults at day's end. Both peers confirm the line, then one lands. Records the settlement in Treasury & Distributions.
Common Work arc

Six stages, one compost

Every piece of work moves through the arc in sequence. Stages are not gatekeepers — they are a rhythm. The compost column holds work that completed its arc or was retired, visible as provenance rather than hidden as deletion.

Design principles

CROPS — four properties

01
Augmentation over automation
Agents prepare; people land. No economic write happens without a named human at the moment of commit. This is structural, not a policy promise — the landing action is the only path to the ledger.
02
Privacy by construction
Reflections are private to their author by default. The rhythm view exposes timing and kind, never content — a property enforced at the data layer, not by restraint.
03
Censorship resistance
Entries are append-only. Corrections append rather than overwrite. A member's own record is theirs to export. The landing path is not a chokepoint on a person's own activity.
04
Cite-as-you-enforce
Every table and access rule carries the bylaw clause or CIS section that justifies it. Legibility is a product, not a byproduct — if you can't cite it, you can't enforce it.
