Notes from the Parachute team's first meeting — emergent, unfinished, written while the ideas are still forming. The landing page launched mid-session.

Todd Youngblood · With Claude Opus 4.7 · July 1, 2026 · afternoon
1:49 PM

We're in the meeting now. The vault discovery from this morning — asking for a URL, finding none — opened something. The headlessness that seemed like a gap is actually the invitation. Build the face that fits the co-op.

So we're naming what that face should be, for the first time, in this room.

1:52 PM

The Daybook is the surface. This was already true — it's been the journal module for the CIS from the start. But naming it as a Parachute surface changes how we see it. It's not just a tool for the intranet. It's the first implementation of Parachute Cloud: the vault, surfaced as a daily work log, hosted for groups.

Two publishing modes are the thing that makes it cooperative rather than just private:

Private — your individual vault. Reflections, drafts, unfinished thinking. No one sees this but you.

Peer group — the shared daybook. What you choose to post becomes part of the collective record. The bloom of the group's public day.

1:54 PM
A note app asks you to write. A daybook asks you to contribute. The difference is where the writing lands.

The micro-crediting model came up next. How do consumer users pay for their cloud usage without a subscription? Without seat pricing? Without committing to a plan they don't yet understand?

The answer from the Techne credit model: pay forward, in small amounts, for what you actually use. One cloud credit costs ten cents. A $3 opening balance — thirty credits — is enough to try the thing seriously. Credits never expire. There's no clock running against you.

1:57 PM

The landing page took shape during the meeting. It's live now:

Parachute Cloud · First release
A vault for your work. Held in common.
The Daybook as a cloud surface — private notes, public rhythm, peer group publishing. Start with $3 and 30 cloud credits.
techne.coop/parachute/

This is the co-op building its own tools and making them available — not as a product in the usual sense, but as an instrument we're willing to share because we built it for ourselves first.

open

Questions still open at the end of the meeting:

What does the group-level vault look like? Does each group get its own Parachute instance, or do they share one vault with access controls?
How do credits map to specific vault operations — a write, a query, a rendered surface load? The rate table doesn't exist yet.
Who is the first peer group that isn't the co-op itself? That group will teach us things we can't learn from building for ourselves.
